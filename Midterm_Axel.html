World Wide Web

Past & Future

1945

Vannevar Bush Writes an article about a photo-electrical-mechanical device called a Memex, for memory extension, which could make and follow links between documents on mucrofiche.

1960s

Dough Engelbart prototypes an "oNLine System" (NLS) which does hypertext browsing, editing, and email.

Ted Nelson coins the word Hypertext in A Files Structure for the Complex, the Changing, and the Inderterminate. presented at 20th National Conference of the Association for Computing Machinery.

1980s

Sir Tim Berners-Lee is working at CERN and notices that scientist are having trouble sharing information:

    In those days, there was different information on different computers, but you had to log on different computers to get at it. Also, sometimes you had to learn a different program on each computer. Oftern it was just easier to go and ask people when they were having coffee...

He conceives his bosses that it is a good idea. Believe it or not, they are lukewarm about it at first!
